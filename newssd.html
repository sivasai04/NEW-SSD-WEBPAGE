About This Course

Software System Development is a graduate-level course that equips students with a comprehensive understanding of the principles, tools, and practices involved in designing and building modern software systems. Emphasizing a systems-oriented perspective, the course explores foundational and contemporary topics including Operating Systems, Computer Networks, Software Engineering methodologies, and Database Systems (both relational and NoSQL).

Students will gain practical experience with industry-relevant tools and technologies such as Bash scripting, SQL, NoSQL databases, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, front-end frameworks, RESTful APIs, Python, and Flask. The course focuses on building scalable, maintainable, and secure software systems while fostering an appreciation for system integration, version control, development workflows, and deployment strategies.

Course Policy

Academic Honesty

Collaboration is for learning, not copying. All submitted work must be your own. Help others learn, don't just provide answers.

Zero Tolerance on Plagiarism

Any form of cheating or plagiarism on assignments, exams, or projects will result in a "0" for that work. Egregious cases will result in a failing grade for the course.

Attendance

Attendance is expected. If you miss a quiz or in-class activity, you will receive a grade of '0' for it. There are no make-up quizzes or activities.

AI Tool Usage

You are free to use AI tools like ChatGPT, but you must cite their use in your submission reports. Remember, these are tools; understanding the core concepts is your responsibility.

Submission Deadlines

No extensions will be granted. Submit your work well before the deadline to avoid last-minute issues. For exemptions due to health or other serious reasons, please work with the academic office.

Miscellaneous

A laptop with a decent configuration is required. Participation in class discussions with the instructor and TAs is highly encouraged.
